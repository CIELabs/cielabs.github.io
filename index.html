Cognitive Innovation in Education Labs (CIEL)

The Cognitive Innovation in Education Lab (CIEL)’s mission is to provide underserved students with high-quality experiential neuroscience education and mentorship experiences such that they can thrive in the high tech economy of tomorrow.

Intro

CIEL’s focus is on training the next generation of critical thinkers from underserved and underrepresented backgrounds to pursue careers in tech, government, and research. We aim to explore the scientific method to teach the value of trial and error, promote critical-reasoning skills. We engage with new technologies, and teach new topics through hands-on experiments.

What do we do?

  • Neuroscience Professional Development for High School Teachers

  • Neuroscience Hardware and Software Design

About Us: Founding Members

Teon L Brooks, PhD (Executive Chair)

Teon joined CIEL while serving as a Data Scientist at Mozilla. Prior to his current role, Teon has worked in the open-source community for 6 years developing software for brain imaging research as well as teaching workshops and tutorials on hands-on experimentation, data acquisition and data analysis. During his graduate work, he received several prestigious research fellowships and awards including the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship, the STEM Chateaubriand Fellowship from the Embassy of France in the US, and was selected as part of the US Delegation to the Lindau Nobel Laureate Meeting for Physiology or Medicine. His Do-It-Yourself Cognitive Science was sponsored by the Fulbright Thailand where he taught 40 students in Bangkok had to build research equipment from open-source hardware, and low-cost electronics Currently living in Brooklyn, NY Teon received his Bachelor of Science in Psychology and Linguistics from The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill (Go Heels!). He received his Master and PhD in cognition and perception from New York University in 2017. He has, He then completed a research fellowship in open science and neuroinformatics at Stanford University.

Mya Doelling (Chief Financial Officer)

Mya joined CIEL while serving as a Senior Director of Rights & Partnerships at United Entertainment Group. Prior to her current role, Mya began her career as Director of Operations for the Michael Phelps Foundation. Her work took her across the globe working with brands including Under Armour, VISA, P&G, Speedo, USA Swimming and the Baltimore Ravens, partners including the Boys & Girls Clubs of America and Special Olympics International as well as to the 2012 Olympic Games in London. Of her many responsibilities, she was responsible for the development of the Foundation in its infancy, monitoring its operations in accordance to Better Business Bureau best practices and maintaining compliance with non-profit tax laws. Mya brings over 10 years of global sponsorship and partnership experience across the global sports and cause marketing landscapes from rights-holder representation, partnership strategy and negotiation, sponsorship portfolio management, talent/influencer marketing, brand strategy, and global event operations. Currently living in Brooklyn, NY Mya holds a BA from Harvard University and an MBA from NYU Stern School of Business with a focus in Marketing and Strategy in 2015.

Steven Azeka (Assistant Director of Development)

Steve joined CIEL while serving as a Curriculum Design Specialist at New York University and an Adjunct at the College of Staten Island. Prior to his current role, Steve taught science, technology, engineering, and mathematics at the elementary and high school levels in California and New York. His academic research interests include the development of online learning environments working for government and nonprofit organizations including the United Nations, Columbia Business School, New York State Education Department, and New York City Department of Education. Steve brings over 10 years of educational experience that includes teaching, curriculum development, professional development, research, and project management. Currently living in Harlem, NY, Steve holds a BS in Electrical Engineering from California Polytechnic State University, an MA from Teachers College, Columbia University and pursuing an EdD from Teachers College, Columbia University with a focus in STEM education.
